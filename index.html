Coffee

Raglan Roast

40 Abel Smith St, Te Aro

Beloved local roaster in a converted garage with second-hand couches and art-covered walls. Students, professionals, and creatives all mingle over some of the best (and cheapest!) coffee in Wellington.

Visit website

Cafes

Floriditas

161 Cuba St, Te Aro

A Wellington institution since 2005. Step into this 1930s-style grand cafe for seasonal European-New Zealand cuisine, famous baking, and an excellent NZ wine list.

Visit website

Pickle & Pie

2 Lombard St, Te Aro

Wellington's take on a NYC deli. House-made pickles, pastrami sandwiches, lox, matzo ball soup, and sweet pies. The pickle jars lining the back wall are available to take home.

Visit website

Best Ugly Bagels

5 Swan Lane, Te Aro

Wood-fired Montreal-style bagels baked fresh on site. Inspired by Saint Viateur in Montreal, these handmade bagels have a unique aroma from the wood-fired oven.

Visit website

Chocolate

Wellington Chocolate Factory

5 Eva St, Te Aro

Award-winning bean-to-bar chocolate made on site since 2013. Watch the making process, enjoy a delicious hot chocolate, or take a tour and make your own bar with custom toppings.

Visit website

Ice Cream

Duck Island

168 Cuba St, Te Aro

24 creative flavours daily in a charming pastel pink space. Try fairy bread, roasted white chocolate and miso, or peppermint slice. Vegan options available too.

Visit website

Craft Beer

Garage Project

68 Aro St, Aro Valley (Taproom at 91 Aro St)

New Zealand's most innovative brewery, born in a converted petrol station in 2011. Over 640 different beers created. Visit the taproom across the road or the cellar door at the original site.

Visit website

Double Vision Brewing

128A Park Rd, Miramar (15 min from CBD)

Award-winning small brewery with playful, boundary-pushing brews. Try Captain Colada (pina colada sour) or Mouth Party (hazy pale ale). "Beer is about friends, family, and fun."

Visit website

Gin

Southward Distilling

35/70 Kingsford Smith St, Lyall Bay

Small batch gin and whisky with bold NZ-inspired flavours. Try Mountain Gin (juniper, citrus, rosemary, lavender) or blend your own botanicals at their tasting room.

Visit website

Wine

Poppies Martinborough

Martinborough (1 hour from Wellington)

Award-winning family winery run by Shayne and Poppy Hammond. Traditional winemaking with a deep respect for the past. Cellar door open daily - tastings $15, refundable with purchase.

Visit website

Restaurants

Chaat Street

171 Willis St, Te Aro

Indian street food tapas that started as a wildly popular pop-up. Bite-sized chaats packed with flavour and spice, plus creative cocktails like chilli margaritas.

Visit website

Kisa

195 Cuba St, Te Aro

Bold Turkish flavours in a 90-seat space centred around NZ's only Josper charcoal BBQ. House-baked pita, mezze platters, and excellent local wine and craft beer.

Visit website

Rosella

18 Marjoribanks St, Mt Victoria

Mediterranean wine bar in an 1886 heritage building. Flavours from across Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. Relaxed neighbourhood vibe with excellent seafood.

Visit website

Rita

89 Aro St, Aro Valley

Intimate 30-seat restaurant in a 100-year-old cottage. Three-course set menu, two sittings per night. Food critic Jesse Mulligan called it his favourite restaurant in the country. Book ahead!

Visit website

Best Wellington Views

Brooklyn Wind Turbine

Top of Brooklyn Hill

NZ's first commercial wind turbine with panoramic views of Wellington Harbour and the South Coast. Walk up from Aro Valley (2 hours) or drive to the top. Information panels and historic WWII bunkers nearby.

Trail info

Te Ahumairangi Hill Lookout

Orangi Kaupapa Rd, Northland

A hidden gem with views as spectacular as Mt Victoria but far fewer crowds. Car park and picnic area at the top. Former site of Wellington's shore-to-ship radio station.

Learn more

Must-Dos

Zealandia Te Mara a Tane

End of Waiapu Rd, Karori (10 min from CBD)

The world's first fully-fenced ecosanctuary - named by TIME as one of the 100 greatest places in the world. See kiwi, tuatara, and rare native birds. Free shuttle from downtown and the Cable Car.

Visit website

Walk along Oriental Parade

Oriental Bay waterfront

Wellington's iconic waterfront promenade. Stroll under heritage Norfolk pines (lit up at night), enjoy harbour views, and relax at the closest beach to the city centre.
